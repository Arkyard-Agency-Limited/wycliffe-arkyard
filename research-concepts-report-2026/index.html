HOTJAR + GA4: HOMEPAGE BEHAVIOUR

Returning visitors aren't here
to donate. They're here to read.

01
With 76% of sessions from returning visitors (GA4), the homepage serves an informed, mission-connected audience. They come to stay close to the work, not to be converted.
02
Hotjar confirms it: click activity concentrates almost entirely in the hero, leading users deeper into stories. Below the fold, including Ways to Give, registers almost no interaction.
03
This is an audience issue, not a UX issue. The homepage does what its audience needs. The solution is reaching new audiences through paid, and landing them on a dedicated campaign page rather than the homepage.

SECTION 03
Insight themes
& campaign concepts.

Four creative concepts built from research. Each one is a testable idea with a clear hook, format direction, and audience target - ready to brief into production.

CONCEPT 01

Give to someone,
not something.

THE INSIGHT

People don't give to causes - they give to people. A named translator, a real story, a specific community. The data is clear: 23% of donors cite personal connection as their primary motivation, and one face-to-camera video drove 34% of all donations.

23%
cite personal worker connection
34%
of donations from one FTC video
FORMAT DIRECTION

Face-to-camera. Named translator. No voiceover. Shot on location or phone. Meta Feed & Reels. Works for both warm retargeting and cold lookalike audiences.

THE HOOK

"I've been learning this language for 7 years. Last week, someone heard a Bible verse in their mother tongue for the very first time."

CREATIVE BRIEF DIRECTION
→Identify 2–3 Wycliffe translators willing to film short face-to-camera pieces
→One specific moment of breakthrough per video - not a general mission statement
→End with a clear, personal ask: "Will you help me keep going?"
→Test 3 variants: different translators, same structure
CONCEPT 02

Did you know?

THE INSIGHT

Cold audiences simply don't know the scale of the problem. That's not a barrier - it's a campaign mechanic. No competitor on Meta is currently running fact-led cold acquisition creative. The gap is wide open.

2,195
languages without a Bible
29%
cite cause belief as #1 motivation
FORMAT DIRECTION

White text on black. Static card or short video. Scroll-stopper open - lead with the stat, pause, then the ask. Pure cold audience on Meta.

THE HOOK

"2,195 languages. No Bible. Not a single word." [pause] "You could change that."

CREATIVE BRIEF DIRECTION
→Lead with the most confronting stat - no warm-up, no context first
→Test static vs. short video (5–10 sec) for scroll-stop performance
→Rotate 3–4 different stat hooks: languages, people, years to translate
→Pair with a direct giving ask - no soft CTA
CONCEPT 03

The content bridge.

THE INSIGHT

The giving journey and the browsing journey are two separate paths. But what if we built a bridge? Words for Life already proves the model - 91.67% of all content sign-ups come from that one offer. The opportunity is to use it as a paid acquisition entry point.

91.7%
of sign-ups from Words for Life
£10k
dedicated testing budget
BUDGET NOTE

This concept uses the £10k testing budget to run a lead-gen funnel: paid sign-up ad → email nurture sequence → giving ask. The goal is to prove whether content-first converts a warm audience over 30–60 days.

THE HOOK

"Get Words for Life - Wycliffe's free magazine. Stories of faith, language, and Bible translation from around the world. Free to sign up."

CREATIVE BRIEF DIRECTION
→Static or carousel ad - lead with a magazine cover or story image
→Optimise for sign-ups, not donations - lower barrier to entry
→3-email nurture sequence post sign-up, ending with a giving ask
→Measure: sign-up CPL, email open rate, donation conversion rate

Fuelling
the moment.

THE INSIGHT

Always-on paid acquisition builds the base. But cultural and faith moments are where you break through. A social-first mindset, led by "Our Kid" Agency, uses these windows to create content that earns reach beyond paid - virality, shares, organic amplification.

How we validate the concepts and what happens next.

TESTING APPROACH

Launch all four.
Let the audience decide.

We launch all four concepts simultaneously within the Advantage+ campaign. Running them together in real conditions gives us a faster and more honest read than a sequential test.

STEP 01
Launch
All four concepts live simultaneously. Arkyard manages setup and campaign structure within the existing Advantage+ framework.
STEP 02
Measure
CPD, CVR, and reach by concept. Benchmarks set before launch. Arkyard provides weekly commentary and performance reporting.
STEP 03
Scale
Review at 4 weeks. Increase budget behind what's working. Learnings from all four inform the next creative cycle.
ADDITIONAL RECOMMENDATIONS

Supporting improvements
for every concept.

01
Connect content to giving. Add contextual CTAs on story and blog pages, and Words for Life prompts within high-engagement content. The audience intent is already there.
02
Reduce payment step friction. The donation form drop-off concentrates at the Direct Debit setup step. Field reduction, mobile trust signals, and a cleaner UI at that point are the targeted improvements.
03
Build the nurture pipeline. A Words for Life subscriber email sequence, return visitor retargeting, and file downloader follow-up. The goal is converting engaged audiences into donors over multiple touchpoints rather than on first contact.
