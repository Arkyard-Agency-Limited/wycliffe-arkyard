Looking ahead — Q3 focus

Ready to test a multi-concept
creative strategy.

To keep momentum and refresh the concepts driving new donor growth, we'll switch on multi-concept campaigns in Q3. Our research points to three directions.

01
People give to people Proven in Q3

Already evident in Q3-to-date performance, leaning on Wycliffe workers as trusted "influencers." People don't give to causes — they give to people. A named translator, a real story, a specific community. The data is clear: 23% of donors cite personal connection as their primary motivation, and one face-to-camera video drove 34% of all donations.

02
Did you know? Open gap

Cold audiences simply don't know the scale of the problem — that's not a barrier, it's a campaign mechanic. No competitor on Meta is currently running fact-led cold acquisition creative. The gap is wide open.

03
The content bridge Testing concept

The giving journey and the browsing journey are two separate paths. But what if we built a bridge? Words for Life already proves the model — 91.67% of all content sign-ups come from that one offer. The opportunity is to use it as a paid acquisition entry point.

Looking ahead — Platform diversification

Spotify audio: a mid-funnel donor test.

Can Spotify audio move warm and warm-adjacent audiences toward the donation journey more efficiently than a cold buy? This is a reach-and-consideration test, measured on signals we can actually see — not gifts that finalise inside Findock.

01 Budget & run
£4,000 / 4 weeks live
Media only — creative is reused, so the cost is in the buy, not the production.
02 Audiences
  • ⅔ Warm lookalike — built from the uploaded donor list. Primary spend.
  • ⅓ Cold contrast — faith / wellbeing interest or contextual playlist.
03 Creative & measurement
Reuse and adapt existing audio with a trusted voice (Gumbel or Scrivener, availability permitting), companion banner with a Donate CTA. Tracked via the Spotify Pixel through GTM — a donate-start proxy event, UTMs into GA4, and a brand-lift survey. Launch only once the pixel is verified and firing.
Ad completion above 90%
CPM benchmarked to radio, not Meta
Positive brand-lift uplift
GA4 lift, warm vs cold cell

Not judged on last-click donations — attribution stays directional while Findock is unresolved.

Looking ahead — Platform diversification

YouTube video: a conversion-led donor test.

Can a Demand Gen campaign, optimised toward the donation signal Google can see, acquire new regular givers more efficiently than the channels where that signal is missing?

01 Budget & run
£5,000 / 5 weeks live
Demand Gen, optimised to conversions. Media only — part of the £10k channel test alongside Spotify.
02 Audiences
Predefined Google audiences (in-market, affinity, life-event) are the workable base. Custom segments & lookalikes are limited — religion is a restricted category, so warm-signal targeting is largely blocked. Creative carries the targeting instead: a trusted-voice film self-selects the right audience.
03 Creative & measurement
Reuse the trusted-voice video shared with Spotify — landscape for in-stream, vertical for Shorts, captions for muted viewing. Bidding optimised to a mid-funnel proxy (donate-start via GTM); the donation signal matches back to the ad click on hashed email, with no change to checkout.
Cost per mid-funnel proxy action
Engaged-view & assisted signals
Brand & search lift
Directional cost per donation

Not judged on last-click alone in the first weeks — the donation signal firms up as tracking beds in.

Looking ahead — Q3 focus

Creative production & refresh — always-on.

An always-on refresh cadence means new creative variants entering testing every few weeks, not once a quarter — feeding the multi-concept strategy above without waiting on a full production cycle each time.

1

Retire underperforming creative on a rolling basis, guided by the leaking/working segments identified this quarter.

2

Batch-produce short-form, face-to-camera content with Our Kid so Instagram and Reels always have fresh supply.

3

Feed learnings from each new concept test — People give to people, Did you know?, The content bridge — straight back into the next production batch.

This always-on cadence is what Our Kid will build against in this afternoon's creative session.

Looking ahead — Q3 focus

New donation flow — more flexible.

Working with Red Bullet to rebuild the donation flow so it can support Apple Pay and Google Pay — both currently blocked by the Findock payment setup. A faster, wallet-enabled checkout should help most at the exact point we know we're losing people: the payment step.

1

Scope the technical path through Findock with Red Bullet to unblock Apple Pay / Google Pay.

2

Prioritise the payment step specifically — the single biggest drop-off point identified in the donation form research.

3

Sequence behind Wycliffe002 (the interactive slider) so the two experiments don't collide in testing.
